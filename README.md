📌 Inventory Demand Forecasting

The Inventory Demand Forecasting project focuses on predicting future product demand for retail stores using historical sales data. Accurate demand forecasting helps retailers maintain optimal inventory levels, minimizing issues such as overstocking and stockouts while improving overall supply chain efficiency

📑 Attribute Information

Date – Represents the specific day of the sales record, used to analyze demand patterns over time.

Store ID – Unique identifier for each store, enabling analysis of location-based demand variations.

Product ID – Unique identifier for each product to track individual sales performance.

Category – Type or group of product, useful for understanding category-level demand trends.

Region – Geographical area of the store, helping capture regional differences in demand.

Inventory Level – Quantity of product available in stock on a given day.

Units Sold – Number of units sold, serving as a key indicator of demand.

Units Ordered – Quantity of product ordered from suppliers for restocking.

Price – Selling price of the product, which influences customer purchasing behavior.

Discount – Price reduction applied to the product, often used to increase sales.

Weather Condition – Environmental factor that may impact buying patterns.

Promotion – Marketing activities or special offers designed to boost product sales.

Competitor Pricing – Pricing strategies of competing stores that influence demand.

Seasonality – Variations in demand due to seasons, festivals, or time-based trends.

Epidemic – External health-related events that may affect consumer behavior.

Demand – Target variable representing the required or predicted quantity of a product.
